Fortis Training

Our Method

Instead of spending thousands on "AAA" hockey with the focus on everything but a player's athletic and personal development, you can spend less, receive more individualized attention, and develop outside the sport. The Fortis method is scientifically driven and all-encompassing. We couldn't possibly detail everything we do here, but we hope it'll give you a good sense of our training philosophy.

Trainining in small groups to maximize gains and individualize our programs: Sick of the factory mill training centers that just run your players through training without correcting form or providing individuals with any attention? So are we. That's why the main core of our program is small group training with 10 athletes or less. We place players together based on their biological age and readiness for different types of training. By having small groups, we get rid of the distractions that often minimize training returns in large settings.

On-ice technical corrections in private or small group skates: It doesn't take us very long to teach and see technical corrections. During these sessions we slow things down and help teach athletes about proper skating technique and edgework.

Camps provide an ideal setting for maximizing repetitions and introducing competition: Our camps are designed to teach all the same skills we work on in small group training. In addition, a larger group allows us to do more competitive small area games and scrimmaging, both important training for any athlete.

Periodization and variation is key: We change up your routine and recommend breaks in training methods to help you achieve maximum training gains. No hockey in March! Rest is key to recover, recover necessary for maximum training gains to be realized.

More than a training program: We would be lying if didn't admit how excited we get when players improve their sports performance. At the same, despite how energized we get when we see gains, it simply isn't enough for us. We want to see our athletes trained by role-models that show them how to act outside of the game, how to lead a team through adversity, and how to help a buddy who is being bullied.
